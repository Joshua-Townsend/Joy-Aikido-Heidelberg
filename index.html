About Aikido

Japanese: 合気道 Hepburn: aikidō? (aikiꜜdoː)

Aikido is a modern Japanese martial art developed by Morihei Ueshiba as a synthesis of his martial studies, philosophy, and religious beliefs. Aikido is often translated as "the way of unifying (with) life energy"[1] or as "the way of harmonious spirit". Ueshiba's goal was to create an art that practitioners could use to defend themselves while also protecting their attacker from injury.

Our Mission
Aikido can help to develop children’s psychomotor skills. But, what is more important, through the practice of Aikido, children will also benefit from the inculcation of values such as self-confidence, discipline, respect, and a sense of care for one another.

Our Aikido Dojo is for people of all ages, including children. We also run joint family training sessions alongside our regular sessions. It's a great way to stay fit and be acitive together as a family.

The Joy Aikido Dojo was founded in January 2017. After many years of Aikido practice in different Aikido dojos and numerous workshops I have fulfilled my dream to open my own dojo.
